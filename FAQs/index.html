To reduce fatigue and boost energy, ensure you're getting adequate sleep, staying hydrated, and fueling your body with nutritious foods. Consider incorporating a balanced mix of carbohydrates, proteins, and healthy fats into your diet. Also, start with moderate exercise and gradually increase intensity to build stamina.

For muscle gains, focus on strength training exercises that target major muscle groups. Increase the weight or resistance gradually as you become stronger. Ensure you're consuming enough protein to support muscle repair and growth. Rest days are crucial for muscle recovery, so include them in your routine.

Ideally, aim for at least 150 minutes of moderate aerobic activity or 75 minutes of vigorous activity spread throughout the week, plus muscle-strengthening activities on 2 or more days a week. Adjust your workout frequency and intensity based on your fitness goals and recovery.

A balanced diet is key to supporting your fitness journey. Focus on whole foods, including vegetables, fruits, lean proteins, whole grains, and healthy fats. Stay hydrated, and consider timing your meals and snacks to fuel your workouts and aid recovery, such as a protein-rich snack after exercise.
